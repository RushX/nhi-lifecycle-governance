# AI Agent Registration Policy
> Version 1.0 | `framework/policies/agent-registration-policy.md`

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes mandatory requirements for the registration of AI agents operating within the enterprise environment. It ensures that every AI agent is formally identified, owned, risk-classified, and governed before it receives access to enterprise systems, data, or infrastructure.

This policy applies to all AI agents regardless of how they were created, who created them, or what platform they run on.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All AI agents, copilots, autonomous services, and AI-powered workflow tools deployed within or connected to enterprise systems
- All employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties who deploy, operate, or manage AI agents on behalf of the organization
- All environments — development, staging, and production
- All AI agent frameworks — including but not limited to LangChain, Microsoft Copilot, AutoGPT, AWS Bedrock agents, and custom-built agents

This policy does not apply to:
- AI features embedded in licensed SaaS products where the organization has no control over the agent's identity or access scope (e.g. built-in AI assistants in Salesforce, ServiceNow)
- Personal productivity AI tools used on personal devices with no connection to enterprise systems

---

## 3. Policy Statement

**No AI agent may connect to, access, or process enterprise data or systems without a valid registration entry in the AI Agent Identity Registry.**

This requirement is absolute. There are no exceptions for development, proof-of-concept, or temporary deployments.

---

## 4. Registration Requirements

### 4.1 Mandatory Registration Attributes

Every AI agent must be registered with the following attributes before deployment:

| Attribute | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Agent name | Human-readable, descriptive name |
| Agent type | One of: copilot, autonomous, api-connected, workflow |
| Owner | Named individual — not a team, shared account, or role |
| Owner email | Current, monitored email address |
| Business unit | Owning department or team |
| Use case | Declared business purpose — specific and bounded |
| Data classification | Highest sensitivity level the agent may access |
| Autonomy level | Scale 1–5 per the Risk Scoring Model |
| External exposure | Boolean — does the agent interact with external systems? |
| Framework | Underlying agent framework or platform |
| Deployment environment | Development, staging, or production |
| End-of-life date | Declared retirement date or renewal trigger |

### 4.2 Auto-Generated Attributes

The following attributes are generated by the registry at registration and cannot be self-reported:

- Unique agent ID (cryptographic UUID)
- Risk score (calculated per the Risk Scoring Model)
- Risk level (Low / Medium / High / Critical)
- Initial status (automatically set to `pending_review`)
- Registration timestamp

### 4.3 Initial Status

Upon successful registration, every agent is automatically placed in `pending_review` status. No agent may transition to `active` status without explicit human certification. This enforces the change approval gate required by ITGC change management controls.

---

## 5. Ownership Requirements

### 5.1 Named Owner

Every AI agent must have a single named human owner at the time of registration. The owner is:

- Accountable for the agent's behavior, access scope, and compliance posture
- Responsible for completing recertification reviews
- The first point of contact for security incidents involving the agent
- Required to acknowledge governance obligations at registration

### 5.2 Owner Restrictions

The following are not permitted as agent owners:

- Shared mailboxes or distribution lists
- Generic role accounts (e.g. "security-team@")
- Contractors without a designated internal sponsor
- Individuals who do not have authority over the agent's declared use case

### 5.3 Owner Departure

When an agent owner departs the organization or changes roles, ownership must be formally reassigned within 48 hours. Agents without a valid owner are automatically suspended pending reassignment.

---

## 6. Approval Requirements by Risk Level

| Risk Level | Score | Required Approvals |
|---|---|---|
| Low | 0–30 | Owner acknowledgment |
| Medium | 31–60 | Owner + team lead sign-off |
| High | 61–80 | Security team sign-off |
| Critical | 81–100 | Security team sign-off + completed threat model |

### 6.1 Threat Model Requirement

Agents with autonomy_level ≥ 3 or data_classification of Confidential or Restricted must have a completed threat model on file before registration is approved. The threat model must address:

- Prompt injection attack vectors
- Data exfiltration risk
- Privilege escalation scenarios
- Blast radius in the event of compromise

---

## 7. Shadow AI

### 7.1 Definition

A shadow AI agent is any AI agent that connects to or processes enterprise data without a valid registry entry. Shadow AI agents are policy violations regardless of intent.

### 7.2 Discovery

The organization will conduct periodic discovery scans to identify unregistered AI agents operating within enterprise systems. Discovery methods include:

- Network traffic analysis for AI API endpoints
- Cloud platform audit logs (AWS, Azure, GCP)
- SaaS application access reviews
- Employee self-reporting programs

### 7.3 Remediation

Upon discovery of a shadow AI agent, the following actions must be taken within 72 hours:

1. Identify the agent's owner or creator
2. Assess the agent's access scope and data exposure
3. Either register the agent retroactively (with full compliance review) or immediately decommission it
4. Document the discovery and remediation in the incident log
5. Assess whether a security incident report is required

Repeated shadow AI violations by the same individual or team are subject to disciplinary action.

---

## 8. Prohibited Registrations

The following agent configurations may not be registered under any circumstances without explicit CISO approval:

- Agents with write access to production databases AND autonomy_level ≥ 4
- Agents processing Restricted data with external_exposure = true AND no human-in-loop checkpoint
- Agents using shared human credentials for authentication
- Agents without a declared end-of-life date or renewal trigger

---

## 10. Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Immediate suspension of the non-compliant agent
- Access revocation for the deploying individual or team
- Escalation to the CISO and legal counsel for significant data exposure
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination for willful violations

---

## 11. Exceptions

Requests for exceptions to this policy must be submitted to the security team with:

- Business justification
- Risk assessment
- Proposed compensating controls
- Time-bound scope (exceptions are not permanent)

Exceptions are logged in the risk register and reviewed quarterly.

---

## 12. Review

This policy is reviewed annually or when significant changes occur to the organization's AI deployment practices or applicable regulatory requirements.
